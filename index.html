Studying Arctic Ice

On July 12 , 2011, crew from the U.S. Coast Guard
Cutter Healy retrieved a canister dropped by
parachute from a C-130, which brought supplies
for some mid-mission fixes. The ICESCAPE, or
“Impacts of Climate on Ecosystems and
Chemistry of the Arctic Pacific Environment,
mission was a NASA shipborne investigation to
study how changing conditions in the Arctic
affect the ocean’s chemistry and ecosystems.
The bulk of the research took place in the
Beaufort and Chukchi seas in summer 2010 and
2011.

Our Changing Planet

For more than 50 years, NASA satellites have provided data on Earth's land, water, air, temperature, and climate. NASA's Earth Information Center allows visitors to see how our planet is changing in six key areas: sea level rise and coastal impacts, health and air quality, wildfires, greenhouse gases, sustainable energy, and agriculture.
